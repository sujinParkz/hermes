RESPONSIBLE
AND
SUSTAINABLE
DEVELOPMENT

Driven by its manufacturing model, Hermès has strengthened its regional presence in France with new production sites and job creations. The company is attentive to ensuring that its economic development is respectful of people and nature.

  • HIGH QUALITY JOBS

    As at 31 December 2020, Hermès employed 16,600 people, of whom 10,383 are based in France and 5,617 are artisans. Over the course of the year, 1,183 people have joined the community of Hermès employees. Hermès objects are manufactured mainly in France, across 51 production sites, including 19 leather workshops (saddlery and leather goods workshops). Looking to the future with confidence, Hermès continues to expand its production capacity by opening new sites on a human scale, each creating 250 new jobs on average.

    Hermès contributes to significantly regenerating the regions in which it opens production sites. The resulting local socio-economic development makes it possible to provide staff with comfortable working conditions combining proximity with stable employment.

  • PRODUCTION SITES IN FRANCE

    Production is carried out in France across 51 sites, organised into regional hubs. This network is completed with twelve manufactures in other countries, located in Switzerland, Italy, the UK, the USA, Vietnam and Australia.

    Through its integrated production model, Hermès carries out 61% of the manufacture of its objects in its own, exclusive workshops. Attentive to the continuous improvement of its artisans’ know-how, the house is involved at the earliest possible stages to guarantee continuity for its métiers. 5,617 artisans in France pass on excellence in artisanal skills to the next generation. Certain sites have training hubs where artisans transmit know-how in ways that are adapted for people with a range of disabilities.

  • RESPONSIBLE DEVELOPMENT

    Hermès systematically seeks long-term and ambitious solutions to better preserve the environment. The sustainability of the group’s activities relies on the availability of high-quality materials, most of which are renewable, and which are at the heart of the house’s uniqueness. Each supply chain is subject to careful monitoring and analysis of its impacts with a view to improvement. Each métier takes specific action to limit its environmental impact.

    Hermès’ artisanal model produces durable objects which are passed down and repaired, echoing the words of Robert Dumas: “Luxury is what can be repaired.”

    Finally, the house is taking action to preserve the planet within the framework of several ambitious commitments. These include the Fashion Pact, which Hermès signed in August 2019, based on action in three main areas: halting climate change, restoring biodiversity and protecting the oceans.

  • A GROUP THAT SUPPORTS STAKEHOLDERS

    Hermès is committed to its suppliers and works with them to address the many challenges they face, from quality, to ethical, social, economic and environmental. The company ensures that its entire value chain is involved in the key sustainable development issues. The quality and longevity of supplier relationships, based on trust built up over time (19 years on average for the main partners), contribute to the robustness of the system.

    The house’s many commitments are depicted by documentary film maker Frédéric Laffont in the “Footprints Across the World” series of films.
